Video lesson 25 is part two of the most frequently used adjectives/adverbs in Dutch. Adjectives are words that are used to describe something (a noun). The can also be used as an adverb if they describe something else (not a noun). Please remind part 1 of the [Dutch adjectives in lesson 9](https://learndutch.org/lessons/adjectives-in-dutch/). Try to use adjectives in your Dutch conversation, as they make the language richer and you are able to give your opinion on something or to describe items more precisely.

> [!note] 
> Please note: this is just a brief introduction (vocabulary). Adjectives can change their form, depending on other words in the sentence and their function. Dutch adjectives sometimes get an extra `[e]`. The number of vowels may change, as well as some consonants. This is explained in detail in our online course [#dutchgrammar](https://www.learndutch.org/dutch-grammar/).
