This video lesson is about questions + interrogative words in dutch. The function of interrogative words is to create a question. Examples are: who, where, what, when, how etc. Most interrogative words have a partner as answer. Such as: there, that, then, like this etc. This lessson deals with both. Dutch interrogative words are pretty much like in English.

Interrogative words are a very important component in every conversation. So learn it well !

(NL) wie:::(EN) who
(NL) wat:::(EN) what
(NL) wat is leuk?:::(EN) what is fun?
(NL) dat:::(EN) that
(NL) Carnaval, dat is leuk!:::(EN) Carnaval, that is fun!
(NL) Wie is dat?:::(EN) Who is that?
(NL) Dat is Rembrandt.:::(EN) That is Rembrandt.
(NL) dit:::(EN) this
(NL) Wat is dit?:::(EN) What is this?
(NL) Dit is kaas!:::(EN) This is cheese!
(NL) welke:::(EN) which
(NL) deze:::(EN) these, this
(NL) die:::(EN) that, those

- dit / deze = this & die / dat = that
- ^^ based on de / het

(NL) waar:::(EN) where
(NL) Waar is Nederland?:::(EN) Where is the Netherlands?
(NL) hier:::(EN) here
(NL) Waar ben je? Hier!:::(EN) Where are you? Here!
(NL) daar:::(EN) there 
(NL) Daar is Nederland!:::(EN) There is the Netherlands!
(NL) wanneer:::(EN) when
(NL) dan:::(EN) then (future + past)
(NL) toen:::(EN) at that time (only in the past)
(NL) terwijl:::(EN) while
(NL) hoe:::(EN) how
(NL) zo!:::(EN) like this!
(NL) hoeveel:::(EN) how much
(NL) de bloem:::(EN) the flower
(NL) Hoeveel bloemen?:::(EN) How many flowers?
(NL) want:::(EN) because
(NL) omdat:::(EN) because

- word order differs between want and omdat
